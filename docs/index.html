It's a live market for one stock

“HELX” is a pretend company. Its price moves whenever someone buys or sells its shares — exactly like a real stock on NASDAQ.

2

The order book = who's waiting to trade

Buyers list the most they'll pay. Sellers list the least they'll accept. It's two lines of people waiting for a deal.

3

A trade happens when they meet

When a buyer's price reaches a seller's price, shares change hands — that's a trade, and it sets the new price. Try it in the panel below!
